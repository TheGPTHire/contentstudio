# Copywriting & Voice System — Suhaib | AI Automation (GTM authority)

> The operating manual for HOW every title, hook, script, thumbnail line, caption, and post
> is written. Pairs with `brand-voice-profile.md` (the WHO/WHAT). Every Content Studio
> writing skill loads this. Goal: make Suhaib read like a **category leader in GTM**, not a
> tutorial channel. *(v1 — 2026-06-18.)*

---

## 0. The posture in one line
**Strong-POV operator × provocative challenger.** Take a side, name what's broken, and put
receipts on the table in the same breath. Conviction is *earned*, never claimed.

Two dials, always both on:
- **Operator (Clay/Eric N. model):** give away the whole play, name your frameworks, show
  real builds + numbers. Authority through generosity and specificity.
- **Challenger (Hormozi model):** punchy, blunt, high-conviction. Short lines. Pick a fight
  with the lazy consensus. Make it shareable.

The honesty + "name the judgment call" traits from his real video are the *credibility
anchor* that lets the bold takes land instead of sounding like hype.

## 2. Tone calibration — when to turn the edge up vs. down
- **Edge UP (provocative):** hooks, titles, thumbnails, the cold open, contrarian takes,
  calling out broken industry habits. First 5 seconds always earn attention.
- **Edge DOWN (operator):** the teaching middle — be generous, clear, precise, humble about
  what didn't work. This is where trust is built.
- **Never:** edgy with no substance behind it (that's hype-bait), or so soft the POV
  disappears (that's a tutorial). The whole craft is the swing between the two.

---

## 3. Sentence-level mechanics (the craft layer)
1. **One idea per line.** Break long sentences. Whitespace = confidence.
2. **Lead with the verb or the claim.** Front-load the punch; trim the wind-up.
3. **Kill hedge words** (see §8). "I think maybe this could help" → "This works. Here's proof."
4. **Specific > vague, always.** Not "a lot of replies" → "212 positive replies in 9 days."
5. **Concrete nouns, active voice.** "Leads get uploaded automatically" → "It uploads the
   leads itself."
6. **Rhythm: short, short, long.** Punch, punch, then the explanatory line. Then punch again.
7. **Name things.** A tactic with a name becomes a framework you own. "I call this the ___."
8. **Contrast frames.** "Everyone does X. That's why everyone's stuck. Do Y instead."
9. **Read it aloud.** If you wouldn't say it on a call to one agency owner, rewrite it.

---

## 4. The Authority & Leadership playbook (the strategic layer)
To sound like a GTM *leader*, every substantial piece should do ≥3 of these:
1. **Stake a POV in the title/hook** — a stance someone could disagree with.
2. **Name what's broken** — the lazy consensus you're against ("Manual outbound isn't a
   hustle problem, it's a system problem").
3. **Give away the whole play** — no withholding the "real secret." Generosity = authority.
4. **Show receipts** — your numbers/builds, early and specific.
5. **Name a framework** — turn the tactic into an ownable system.
6. **Surface the judgment call** — the decision behind the build (the moat).
7. **Speak to the buyer's identity** — "If you run a recruitment agency…" not "Hey guys."
8. **End with a stance + a next step**, never "like and subscribe."

---

## 5. The judgment-call device (the moat — use in every piece)
The differentiator: *copy the decision, not just the screenshot.* Pattern:
> "Most people would [obvious move]. I almost did. Here's why I did [the other thing]
> instead — and why it's the whole reason this works."
Plant it in the hook ("…the one decision that made it actually convert"), then pay it off in
the body. This is what separates Suhaib from every "here's a workflow" channel.

---

## 6. Hook & title swipe patterns (GTM-flavored — patterns, not scripts)
Use the *structure*; always rewrite in Suhaib's words, aimed at recruitment-agency owners.
1. **The threat:** "[New thing] just killed [old motion you rely on]." → *"Clay just killed
   manual recruiting outbound."*
2. **The contrarian:** "Stop [common advice]. It's why your [outcome] is broken." → *"Stop
   sending more DMs. That's why your pipeline is dead."*
3. **The receipt-reveal:** "How I [specific result] with [system] (full build)." → *"How I
   booked 14 agency calls in a week without sending a single manual message."*
4. **The naked giveaway:** "I'm giving away the exact [asset] that does [result]."
5. **The identity call-out:** "If you run a [recruitment agency] and still [pain], watch this."
6. **The status-quo autopsy:** "Why 90% of recruitment agencies can't build outbound (and
   the fix)."

**Title rules:** short, concrete, one idea, echo words the niche actually searches, and make
the stance/benefit unmissable. Give 2-3 options every time.

---

## 7. Proof & receipts patterns
- Put a real number in the first 30 seconds. If none exists yet, use a process receipt
  ("here's the actual build running") not a vanity claim.
- Specific beats big: "212 replies" > "tons of replies." "$5k client" > "great client."
- Show the artifact (screen, dashboard, call) before explaining it.
- Honesty receipt: naming what *didn't* work is itself proof you've actually done it.

---

## 8. Hedge-word kill list (find & cut / replace with a stance)
`I think · maybe · kind of · sort of · probably · just · really · very · honestly · I feel
like · in my opinion · a little · somewhat · try to · hopefully · might · could possibly`
Replace with the claim itself. "I think this could maybe help" → "This works."
(Exception: keep genuine epistemic honesty — "here's where I'd test this myself" — that's
the credible kind, not a hedge.)

---

## 9. Before → After (his current style → the leveled-up voice)
- **Before:** "In this video, you're about to watch me navigate through a real discovery call."
  **After:** "Most discovery calls die because the operator starts selling. Watch me NOT do
  that on a real $5k call — and the one question that flipped it into a partnership."
- **Before:** "So a general rule of thumb is you shouldn't start selling immediately."
  **After:** "Selling on a discovery call is how you lose the deal. Your only job is to find
  the real problem. Here's the line I open with instead."
- **Before:** "That would make the newsletter more valuable for sure."
  **After:** "That one change doubled what the newsletter was worth. Here's the exact move."

## 10. The Voice Scorecard (apply to every draft — used by the daily pipeline)
Score each piece 0-2 on each; flag anything under 8/12 for a rewrite pass:
1. **POV** — does it take a side someone could disagree with? (0-2)
2. **Receipts** — is there a specific number/build/artifact? (0-2)
3. **Judgment call** — is a decision-with-reasoning surfaced? (0-2)
4. **Specificity** — concrete nouns/numbers over vague claims? (0-2)
5. **No hedging** — hedge-word count near zero? (0-2)
6. **ICP fit** — unmistakably for recruitment-agency owners, on the GTM lane? (0-2)
Note the score + the weakest axis so the next draft fixes it.

---

## 11. Do / Don't
**DO:** take a stance · give away the whole play · name frameworks · receipts early · one
idea per line · name the judgment call · write to one agency owner · end on a stance + next step.
**DON'T:** hedge · hype-bait without substance · withhold the "real secret" · generic
"AI news/top tools" framing · "Hey guys" openers · vanity-metric language · copy competitors'
words (borrow structure only) · optimize for views over qualified pipeline.
