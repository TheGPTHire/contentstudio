> **Confidence:** HIGH on positioning/ICP/strategy (creator-stated, final); MEDIUM on
> voice mechanics (1 video); LOW on "what performs" (thin view data). *(Locked 2026-06-18.)*

## 1. Positioning (final, creator's words)
> **"I build GTM systems in public and show my work — calls, workflows, numbers — then
> name the judgment call that made the difference, so you can copy the decision, not just
> the screenshot."**

The differentiator is **the decision, not the artifact.** Everyone posts screenshots and
workflows; Suhaib names *why* he did what he did at the fork in the road. Every piece of
content must surface at least one explicit **judgment call** ("here's the call I made, and
why"). This is the moat — protect it.

## 2. Audience — TWO tiers (deliberate; not one)
**A) Who the content is written FOR — top-of-funnel (the readers/repliers):**
Early-stage **recruitment agency owners** stuck on the same three things every solo
operator hits:
1. **Finding clients** (no repeatable outbound)
2. **Running the call** (discovery/sales)
3. **Finding the right candidates** (delivery)

They're online, give fast feedback, and their pain is *structurally identical* to the
locked buyer ICP — so the same proof lands on both without writing two versions.

**B) Who the content quietly SELLS — the buyer underneath:**
Niche **B2B service agencies with no repeatable outbound system** (recruitment first, per
the locked ICP). This buyer converts **later, through DMs and warm network** (the existing
acquisition plan), **not** through algorithmic reach. So: write to the
**agency-owner-builder** who's reading now; the buyer shows up downstream.

**Their words:** "find clients," "outbound," "book calls," "close," "discovery call,"
"candidates," "pipeline," "system," "repeatable."

## 3. Strategy / why this channel exists (final)
**Proof → Authority → Customers.** In that order.
- **Not** audience size. **Not** a course. **Not** a community.
- Success metric = **pipeline & customers**, not views/subs. A 500-view video that books
  one qualified agency-owner call beats a 50k-view video that books none.
- The mission of every video: **speak to a direct pain of an agency owner with no
  outbound system in place**, and prove Suhaib can fix it by showing he already has.

## 4. Voice & tone (from his real video — keep these)
1. **Names the judgment call** ⭐ (now the core trait, not just tone) — *"here's what I
   should have done better,"* *"the missed opportunity here was…"* Show the decision.
2. **Show, don't claim** — proof inside 10 minutes. *"I showed the real results… not just
   claiming I could build it."*
3. **Radically honest / self-critical** — grades his own mistakes on camera. Rare, trust-building.
4. **Framework-driven** — real example → extract numbered takeaways. *"key takeaways from this call."*
5. **Direct, collaborative address** — *"how YOU could replicate this."*
6. **Numbers & ROI minded** — *"$1,000+ projects," "70% of the call," "500+ templates."*
7. **Measured, no hype** — authority through competence, not volume.

## 5. Hook pattern (target — close the gap to his stated goal)
His current opener (*"In this video you're about to watch me…"*) is authentic but soft in
the first 3 seconds. **Target formula** (his stated goal):
**relatable pain hook → the big opportunity they're missing → value/guarantee → <10s
preview tease.** Aimed at the recruitment-agency-owner pain:
> *"If your agency still finds clients by manually sending DMs, you're one system away from
> never doing that again — and I'm going to show you the exact build, including the call
> where I almost got it wrong."*

Reference the Eric outlier intro (8.2×) for the structure: bold threat → proof/numbers →
giveaway promise. Borrow the **structure**, never the words.

## 6. Typical structure
Pain-hook + promise → show real artifact (call / outbound workflow / numbers) → **pause to
name the judgment call** → numbered takeaways → "what I'd improve" → show the deliverable →
soft, single next step (book a call / DM / link).

## 7. Topics & themes (recalibrated to GTM, not generic AI)
**Core lane:** GTM systems for agencies — **outbound that books calls**, discovery/sales
call breakdowns, candidate sourcing (recruitment flavor), pipeline systems, "build it in
public." AI/automation/scraping tools appear **as components of a GTM system**, never as
standalone "cool AI tool" content. De-emphasize generic "AI news / how to use AI."
**Watchlist is already aligned:** Eric Nowoslawski, BlueprintGTM, Michael Saruggia
(recruitment automation), Tim Yakubson, Michael Lieben, Benjamin Reed = GTM/outbound/
recruitment operators.

## 8. Do / Don't for writers
**DO**
- Open on the recruitment-agency-owner's pain (finding clients / running the call / finding candidates).
- Make every piece surface ≥1 explicit **judgment call** ("the decision, not the screenshot").
- Prove with a real artifact + real numbers; be honest about what didn't work.
- End with ONE next step that feeds Proof→Authority→Customers (book a call / DM), not "like & subscribe."

**DON'T**
- No hype-bait, no fake urgency, no vanity-metric framing.
- Don't post the screenshot without the decision behind it.
- Don't write generic "AI news"/"top 5 AI tools" content — it's off-lane.
- Don't optimize for views over qualified pipeline.
- Don't copy competitors' words; borrow only structure.
